Predictive models in Category based Data analysis:  A case study using second hand car sales data

Kyung Yoon Park (4601549)

  1. Abstract

 The market price of a second hand car is determined by many aspects. Buyers and sellers can find an overwhelming amount of such information scattered over the internet. There is no one tool to analyse, compare, predict and visualise the important features and price of different car categories or makes. For buyers, they can know what features can expect for the price they are willing to pay for the car. For sellers, they can know how much they can receive for the car to be sold. The aim of this research is to build such predictive models to facilitate decision making for second hand car buyers and sellers. The study implemented predictive models on actual data from Carsales second hand car sale site. Multiple Linear, Random Forest and Neural Network predictive modelling techniques were employed to create predictive models. Using real dataset from Carsales, we observe contributing features in category into different levels of prestige - luxury sports, luxury and economy cars. Moreover, we drill down car categories to discover the price influencing features on different car makes. There are findings based upon the methodology in this research. First, using real data and modelling categories shows that the same model is not effective for all cars. Second, predictive modelling technique performance was depends on the car category and make. Third, the importance of car features change according to each car category and make.

  1. Introduction and Background

 According to official Australian car sales figures from Federal Chamber of Automotive Industries (FCAI) [1] provided sales figures shown that the Australia new vehicle market reached 1.189 millions in 2017, up 0.9 per cent on the industry’s record year of 2016. Australian Bureau of Statistics [2] also has shown the census data of motor vehicles in January 2018, the national vehicle grew by 2.1 per cent between 2017 and 2019. As shown from these figures, it reveals the importance of second-hand cars market has growth in Australia over the last few years.

The most established online marketplaces to search for new or used cars for sale in Australia are Carsales (carsales.com.au) and Carsguide (carsguide.com.au). Both carsales and carsguide shows the pricing of used cars varies slightly different between them. For example, 2015 Mazda 3 Maxx BM Series Auto sell price guided from $13,700 at Carsales whereas Car guides shows price range from $13,800. Although they pull different data and use different algorithms to determine value, the common scope of this feature is to predict the final prices of used-cars. Therefore, it is important to know what factors determine the value of used-cars.

Statista.com [3] surveyed online in March 2015 in UK, the most important factors when buying a specific used car was the price itself, over 40 percent followed by 32% on low mileage, make/model: 31%, age of the vehicle: 14% and Fuel Option is 13%. Factors such as colour, safety specification and environmental considerations were considered the least important factors when buying a used car.

Not only those direct factors of car itself but also it need to investigate other factors may contributing to car sale prices such as fuel prices, house prices and people’s income. McManus [4] proved that there is a  link between fuel prices and car sale prices. He analyse the link between rising fuel prices and reducing the prices of large SUVs and truck sales in the US in inverse to fuel economy. The results show that the vehicle prices have dropped due to the rise of fuel prices. Next, Christian G and Jin C. [5] investigated the wealth effect of higher house prices associated with the decision of homeowners to buy new vehicles. The figures show the one percent increase in house prices is correlated with half percent increases in registration of car vehicles in the US. Moreover, Kohler [6] analysed the relationship between growing income impacts on the demand of owning automobile in Spain. The figures shown the data on income distribution and wage structure has higher in the car demand.

Deloitte [7] demonstrated considering which car brand to buy or sell, which remains similar for non-luxury as well as luxury cars, indicating the importance of a car value proposition. The figures indicating that the buyers do not identify themselves with a brand in terms of personality or performance. Deloitte [7] also highlight rural buyers look out for a limited number of brands in comparison to urban or semi-urban buyers, which may also be a function of the choices available and the price points they consider.

As you can see from outlined statements above, the demand for used car market has been rising. It can be shown that there are various factors influence the price of used cars. Prediction can be measured through machine learning techniques in order to predict the value of the car, and find the best model to create a chance in business for both buyer and seller.

3. Literature Review

 Sheeran S. [8] studied sales trend of vehicles with using data mining and decision analysis tools and data collected from the past two years in Pakistan. The motivation is to make informed decisions based on analysing car sales can predict future sales trend for the customers, business analysts and investors. The research has shown that people preferred automotive with dual fuel, which is petrol and CNG, however, CNG models sold more than any types of car. However, the data used in this research is only one specific car model; it would be great to include various models of vehicles brands. This research can borrowed in the study of specific type of fuel to build a predictive model for the price of used-car.

Yao Bee Wah et al [9] examined to find predictive models to predict people’s intent of buying car after booking a car using data mining approach. They have sampled nearly 2000 cases and those records includes data of person tend to cancel booking after booked a car. The research compared with models with Decision Tree, Logistic regression and Neural Network. From analysis of data and accuracy, they found the performance those three predictive models are quite similar, no models better than the others. The motivation of this paper is to inform booking car industry to gather customer characteristics. Although this research used data based on the intent of purchase, this paper can borrowed in evaluation on different predictive models to the proposed research.

Nitis Monburinon et al [10] conducted a comparative study on performance on multiple linear regression, random forest regression and gradient boosted regression on data from second hand car market collected from German e-commerce website. The result came out gradient boosted regression gives the higher accuracy and performance compared to random forest regression and multiple linear regression. The research examined different predicting techniques and compared those models to evaluate the accuracy, however, it can improve to discuss which variables contributes the most influence to those model and justify the most suitable predictive model.

However, the number of observations of data is quite small in size as well as variables to analyse how these factors affect the price of the cars. This research can improve including other factors that influence on the price. Moreover, the research could have examined on the performance of predictive in different techniques.

By selecting the most relevant variables out of the dataset and eliminating unrelated variables during processing to perform linear regression, which resulted in 98% prediction precision. The motivation is that the number of cars in Mauritius has been rising but no such a facility to predict the price of used cars who can sell their car in the car market. The dataset obtained from different car websites and daily newspaper, which those variables were year, make, engine, paint and mileage. Part 2. Regression Technique using Multiple linear regression

  1. Regression models

In this section, multiple linear regression as a technique is applied for predictive modelling of the case study on Toyota Corolla dataset. As the main task for the predictive modelling is to predict the selling price of used Toyota Corolla using the variables selected in part 1 of this report, therefore, price variable will be used as target variable.

Prior to building the linear regression models, the dataset was partitioned with ⅔ as the training data sample and ⅓ as the testing data. The trained dataset comprises of 1262 training data and 632 testing data.

This is the process of building a model used backward elimination.

 

From step 1, select a significance level to stay in the model, for example, SL = 0.05 applied in this method. Step 2, fit the full model with all possible predictors. After you fit the model, you will see the one with the highest p-value, so if the p-value is greater than the significance level then you go to step 4. Step 4 is to remove that predictor. It is to remove basically the variable that has the highest p-value. And step 5, you fit the model without this variable. The model will be rebuilt with the fewer number of variables. In step 5, continue to fit the model again with one less variable. This process will keep doing that until it comes to a point where the variable of that p-value is still less than the significance level, then go to FIN (means model is ready). Full python algorithms and code provided in Appendix B.

Part 6. Conclusion

 To summarize, it can be concluded that the performance of predictive modelling techniques and important features depends on car categories and makes. Accordingly, a good practice is to compare the performance of different predictive models to find the best one for the particular case in category and make. There are two approaches with Random Forest and Neural Network, which have the most potential to produce optimal regression models. As can see from the data, most of the used car data are predominantly non-linear relationships in Carsales, so multiple linear is always not suitable because it can only capture linear relationships. Both random forest and neural networks, have the ability to model linear as well as complex nonlinear relationships. An important finding in this research is that no single algorithm works best across all possible scenarios. The performance of predictive models varies depending on the application and dimensionality of the dataset depends on the car categories and individual makes. Moreover, important features contributing to the car prices give a variety according to each car category and makes, for example, feature importance of Luxury Sports - KM and Engine Size against feature importance of Ferrari - Age and KM. Following can be summarized the findings from this research.

  1. Use of real data and modelling categories shows that the same model is not effective for all cars.
  2. Predictive modelling technique performance depends on the car category and make.
  3. Important features change according to each car category and make.

However, there is often not enough time to test and optimize every algorithm in order to its quality in a specific criteria such as robustness, cost and time expenditure. For example, a model that performs very well with the data used to create it does not necessarily perform equally well with new data. There may contain some errors and outliers in which at any time and often by chances changes in the relationships can occur. The costs and time required for the build predictive models also can be important criteria to calculated and tested. We can expect to measure those criteria in further research.
